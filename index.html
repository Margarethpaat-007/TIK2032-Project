Toyota Starlet

The Toyota Starlet is a legendary compact hatchback produced by Toyota between 1973 and 1999. Known for its stylish simplicity, excellent fuel efficiency, and agile handling, the Starlet quickly became a city favorite. In Indonesia, it gained iconic status in the 1980s and 1990s, especially with the beloved EP70 and EP80 series. Even today, the Toyota Starlet remains a nostalgic classic, loved by retro car enthusiasts and modification fans alike for its timeless charm and endless customization potential.

Suzuki Jimny

The Suzuki Jimny is a compact off-road SUV known for its rugged design, lightweight body, and impressive off-road capability. Launched in 2018, this fourth-generation Jimny keeps its classic boxy look while adding modern features and improved performance. Loved for its go-anywhere attitude and iconic styling, the JB74 is a favorite among adventure seekers and off-road enthusiasts around the world.

Honda Estilo

The Honda Estilo is a sporty hatchback variant of the Honda Civic, produced in the early 1990s. Known for its sleek, aerodynamic design and agile handling, the Estilo became popular among car enthusiasts, especially for its potential in modifications and street performance. In Indonesia, it’s considered a classic and remains a favorite in the retro and tuner car communities.

The Rise of Electric Vehicles (EVs)

Electric cars like Tesla Model 3 and Hyundai Ioniq 5 are changing the automotive world. They’re clean, quiet, and fast. As charging stations grow, more people are switching to EVs for a greener, futuristic driving experience.

Why Sedans Still Matter?

Even with SUVs dominating the market, sedans like the Honda Civic and Toyota Camry still have loyal fans. Their smooth handling, fuel efficiency, and sleek designs make them a reliable choice for daily commuting and long drives.

Why You Should Change Your Car Oil Regularly?

Oil is like blood for your car’s engine. If you skip oil changes, the engine wears out faster, gets noisy, and might overheat. Ideally, change your oil every 5,000–10,000 km depending on the type and usage. Don’t wait until it breaks down!
